Exponent Rules

Logarithmic Exponent Rule


$$ \begin{align*} a^m &= a^n \\ \ln{\left(a^m \right)} &= \ln{\left(a^n\right)} \\ \ln{(a)} \cdot m &= \ln{(a)} \cdot n \\ \frac{\ln{(a)}}{\ln{(a)}} \cdot m &= \frac{\ln{(a)}}{\ln{(a)}} \cdot n \\ \therefore m &= n \end{align*} $$

If the bases are the same on either side of the equals sign then the exponents are equal to each other.

\begin{align*}
    a^m &= a^n \\
    \ln{\left(a^m \right)} &= \ln{\left(a^n\right)} \\
    \ln{(a)} \cdot m &= \ln{(a)} \cdot n \\
    \frac{\ln{(a)}}{\ln{(a)}} \cdot m &= \frac{\ln{(a)}}{\ln{(a)}} \cdot n \\
    \therefore m &= n
\end{align*}
